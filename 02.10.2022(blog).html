DEHRADUN

Dehradun Travel Guide: A Traveler’s Handbook To Explore The Capital City Of Uttarakhand!



Dehradun or the “Home of the valley”, is a beautiful hill station in Uttarakhand which is enclosed by the mighty ranges of Garhwal Himalayas. This capital city is located at an altitude of 1400 feet and offers a great experience to the adventure junkies, couples and families. A home to beautiful natural caves and magnificent waterfalls, this is a relaxing escape for the people planning to get away for the weekend. As the heat is already rising up, take a quick break to this scenic city and don’t miss out on the best things by keeping this handy Dehradun travel guide.







Best Time To Visit



The time between March-June is the best to plan a vacation to Dehradun. During this time the city experiences a huge flock of people who visit this place to enjoy the pleasant weather. While the weather is favorable for tourists, there is a scope of exploring the best sights and indulge into some adventurous activities during this time. The temperature reaches to a maximum of 35 degrees celsius and the minimum temperature is 17 degrees.





How To Reach

By Road

There are many private buses which runs between Delhi to Dehradun and takes at least 5-6 hours to reach. Apart from that the travelers can drive their car from Delhi passing through the NH72A, to reach the city.

By Flight

The nearest airport to Dehradun is the Jolly Grant airport which is located at a distance of 20 miles from the main city. One can reach the city by hiring a taxi which will cost around 2,000 approximately.

By Train

Dehradun is a major station in many train lines and the time may vary according to the origin station.


Must Place To See

While you’re in Dehradun, make sure to not miss out any of these places which are located in the proximity of the city.


1. Sahastradhara



The literal meaning of Sahastradhara is “Thousand fold spring”, located at a distance of 14 Kms from the Dehradun city. This is one of the famous places to visit in Dehradun which is known for its natural caves and waterfalls. Apart from this, Sahastradhara is also known for its medicinal and therapeutic values. Numerous water pools surrounds this place in which the water drips from limestone stalactites, turning into sulphur pools. 2. Robber’s Cave



Robber’s cave or Gucchu Pani is one of the best places to visit in Dehradun. Located 8 Kms away from the main city at the Anarwala village, this place attracts the adventure seekers and couples who want to escape the unbearable heat. This place is famous for its natural caves which are filled with cold water offering a unique experience. The Robber’s cave name was given by Britishers after they discovered that the robbers used to hide at this place after robbery.
